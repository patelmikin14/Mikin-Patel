**About Me**

I’ve always been the kind of person who can’t leave things the way they are. I take them apart, understand how they work, and figure out how to make them better. I focus on improving, optimizing, testing and when needed, fixing. For me, building something reliable isn’t just about making it work, it’s about understanding how it might fail, catching those problems early, and designing around them.

I’m exploring PCB DFM and working on projects to understand what really goes on behind the scenes from design decisions to manufacturing constraints and why some designs succeed while others don’t.

This GitHub is my personal workspace. It’s where I put everything I’m learning, building, and figuring out along the way. If you’re here, feel free to explore, question things, or share feedback. **I’m here to grow, and I believe the best way to do that is with others.**
